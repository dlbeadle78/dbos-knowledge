# Leader in Adult Care v1.2 — Professional Discussion Guide

## Scope

This file records public assessment information for the **Leader in Adult Care apprenticeship standard, ST0008, version 1.2**.

Version 1.2 applies to apprenticeship starts from **22 April 2026**. Earlier starts may be attached to version 1.1 or version 1.0 and must be assessed against the correct assessment plan.

## Assessment method

The professional discussion is a structured assessment with an independent assessor.

- Duration: **90 minutes**
- The apprentice must be given the opportunity to demonstrate the mapped knowledge, skills and behaviours to the highest available grade.
- The discussion must cover every mapped topic group.
- Workplace examples should be authentic, sufficiently detailed and linked to the apprentice's own leadership practice.

## Topic groups

The published assessment plan groups the professional discussion content under the following headings:

1. **Tasks and responsibilities**
2. **Dignity and human rights**
3. **Communication**
4. **Safeguarding**
5. **Health and wellbeing**
6. **Professional development**
7. **Leadership**

## Typical evidence expected within each group

### Tasks and responsibilities

Evidence may include:

- applying statutory frameworks, standards, guidance and codes of practice
- maintaining regulatory and organisational compliance
- risk assessment and outcome-based practice
- balancing individual rights with duty of care
- quality monitoring and service improvement
- change management
- performance management, appraisal and disciplinary processes
- managing resources effectively

### Dignity and human rights

Evidence may include:

- equality, diversity and inclusion
- dignity, rights and fairness
- person-centred practice
- active participation and co-production
- challenging discriminatory or restrictive practice

### Communication

Evidence may include:

- communication with individuals, families, staff and professionals
- confidentiality and lawful information sharing
- recording and reporting systems
- communication tools and technology
- evaluating whether communication systems are effective

### Safeguarding

Evidence may include:

- adult and child safeguarding legislation and local arrangements
- recognising and responding to abuse or unsafe practice
- reporting and escalation
- staff training and support
- whistleblowing culture
- monitoring whether safeguarding systems remain effective

### Health and wellbeing

Evidence may include:

- monitoring and responding to changes in health and wellbeing
- health and safety leadership
- risk-management culture
- reviewing and improving policies and practice
- supporting staff wellbeing where this affects safe and effective care

### Professional development

Evidence may include:

- supervision and reflective practice
- identifying learning and development needs
- planning, recording and evaluating development activity
- research and evidence-based practice
- current developments in adult social care
- demonstrating how development has improved practice or performance

### Leadership

Evidence may include:

- leadership and management theories
- effective team performance
- values-based culture
- own behaviour and impact on others
- innovation and initiative
- recognising the contribution and strengths of others
- leading change and sustaining improvement

## Pass standard

To achieve a pass in the professional discussion, the apprentice must meet **all pass criteria** mapped to this assessment method.

Pass-level evidence normally needs to:

- explain relevant legislation, guidance, systems, theories or processes accurately
- show how these were applied in the apprentice's own service
- provide genuine workplace examples
- explain decisions and the rationale behind them
- demonstrate monitoring of quality, compliance, safeguarding, staff performance and outcomes
- show how actions supported people receiving care and support

A response that only describes policy or theory, without showing application in the apprentice's own work, is unlikely to provide sufficient evidence.

## Distinction standard

The apprentice must first meet all pass criteria.

For version 1.2, the professional discussion distinction threshold is **at least 19 of the 24 distinction criteria**.

Distinction-level evidence goes beyond describing what happened. It normally includes:

- analysis of why an approach was chosen
- critical evaluation of legislation, guidance, systems or practice
- evaluation of the effectiveness and impact of decisions
- comparison of intended and actual outcomes
- identification of weaknesses, risks or lessons learned
- evidence that monitoring led to measurable improvement
- explanation of how practice was reviewed and adapted
- evidence of sustained influence across the service rather than a single isolated action

## Recommended answer structure

A useful structure for each example is:

> **Situation → responsibility → action → rationale → legislation or theory → outcome → evidence of impact → evaluation → subsequent improvement**

The apprentice should be ready to explain not only what they did, but why they did it, how they know it worked, what they learned and what changed afterwards.

## Overall grading relationship

The observation is graded **fail or pass**.

To achieve an overall distinction, the apprentice must:

- pass the observation, and
- achieve a distinction in the professional discussion.
